Direction A: Blueprint Proposal

Instruction manual aesthetic — clean outlines, flat fills, technical precision

Direction A: Blueprint

Everything gets clean outlines — like a LEGO instruction manual page. Flat fills, no gradients. A subtle background grid reinforces the technical-drawing feel. The result is precise, controlled, and distinctly "LEGO instructions."

Outlines on all elements Flat fills (no gradients) Subtle grid overlay White windows Road shadow removed Same composition Flatter sky
  • Changed: All elements have 1.5–2px strokes in darker shade of their fill. Flat colors replace gradients. Windows are white (light from inside).
  • Kept: Same layout/composition, lollipop tree shape, mountain triangles, building proportions
  • Prove-it: Sky is flat pale blue instead of gradient — might feel too washed out?
Direction B: Brick by Brick Proposal

Everything is visibly built from LEGO bricks — stepped shapes, studs, baseplate ground

Direction B: Brick by Brick

Everything is visibly constructed from LEGO bricks and plates. Mountains are stepped pyramids, trees are stacked round plates, buildings show brick course lines and roof studs. The ground is a baseplate with a subtle stud grid. The most "toy-like" and unmistakably LEGO.

Stepped brick mountains Stacked-plate trees Brick courses on buildings Roof studs Baseplate ground Plate clouds Sun with studs Brick wall fence Rectangular pond Road shadow removed Sky gradient Grass gradient
  • Changed: All organic shapes replaced with blocky/angular LEGO brick equivalents. Visible studs on top surfaces. Baseplate stud pattern on ground.
  • Kept: Sky and grass gradients unchanged. Road surface unchanged. LEGO brand colors on buildings unchanged.
  • Strongest LEGO identity — most dramatic departure from current style.
Direction C: LEGO Land Proposal

Polished, vibrant, enriched — depth shadows, strategic studs, decorative extras

Direction C: LEGO Land

The most polished option — keeps current organic shapes but adds depth shadows (matching the design system's box-shadow pattern), strategic stud accents, warm sun glow, and new decorative elements (flowers, signpost). Feels like LEGO promotional art — vibrant and enriched without losing familiarity.

Depth shadows on elements Sun warm glow Stud accents on trees/buildings Mountain plate bands Subtle grass stud pattern Road shadow removed Decorative flowers Signpost Sky unchanged Grass gradient unchanged Cloud shape unchanged Fence unchanged Tree shape unchanged
  • Changed: Depth shadows behind mountains, buildings, trees, pond, windmill. Stud circles on tree canopies and building rooftops. Mountain plate-layer lines. Sun glow halos. Subtle baseplate dot pattern on grass.
  • New: 4 LEGO-style flowers (round 1×1 plates on stems), directional signpost
  • Kept: Sky gradient, grass gradient, cloud shapes, tree shapes, fence style — all unchanged. Least disruptive of the three directions.
