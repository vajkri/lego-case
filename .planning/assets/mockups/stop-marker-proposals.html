Stop Marker Proposals

5 story-driven designs for map stop indicators. The presentation is a LEGO instruction booklet: each stop is a build step in the migration journey from rural AngularJS to the React city. Labels flip above/below based on road slope to avoid overlapping the road or car.

Instruction Step Numbers

Each stop is a numbered step in the LEGO instruction booklet — exactly like the circled step numbers in real LEGO manuals. The audience follows along as the plan is built, one step at a time. Visited steps get a checkmark.

Story: "You're following a LEGO instruction manual. Each numbered step builds the case. Complete all 5 steps to finish the build."

LEGO Road Signs

Miniature road signs planted alongside the road — like the sign accessories in LEGO City sets. The label IS the marker (no separate indicator + label). Clean, self-contained, fits the road-trip metaphor perfectly.

Story: "You're on a road trip. Each sign marks a destination on the route. Grey signs are ahead, yellow is where you are now, green signs are behind you."

Label Positioning Rule

  • Road going uphill (ascending left-to-right) — label below the marker to avoid overlapping the road/car ahead
  • Road going downhill (descending left-to-right) — label above the marker to stay clear of the road below
  • Computed per-stop from the road path derivative at that offset position
  • Proposal 5 (Road Signs) integrates label into the marker itself, so the rule applies to the whole sign placement
