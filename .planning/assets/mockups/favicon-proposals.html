Favicon Proposals

Two favicon designs for the LEGO Migration Proposal deck. Both draw from existing stop marker designs — Build Progress Bricks and Minifig Journey Heads — simplified for 16×16 to 64×64 pixel rendering.

1

Mini-bricks

Two stacked LEGO bricks with studs on a green baseplate — the act of building, frozen in icon form. Yellow on red mirrors the active stop marker palette. Instantly reads as "LEGO" at any size.

Inspired by: Build Progress Bricks stop marker (#2) — "Each stop adds to the build. Brick by brick."

Design notes
  • Two-brick stack: yellow top (#F5CD2D) + red bottom (#E3000B) — same palette as active stop markers
  • Green baseplate (#58CC3A) grounds the composition and adds a third recognizable LEGO element
  • Studs on the top brick are the key recognition cue — they survive even at 16px as small bumps
  • Each element has a 1–2px darker offset underneath for subtle depth, matching the brick aesthetic of the stop markers
  • No outline needed — the multicolor silhouette is distinctive on any background
2

Engineer Head

A LEGO minifig head wearing a red hard hat — the builder presenting the migration plan. Friendly and confident expression. The yellow-and-red combination is immediately recognizable as LEGO even at tiny sizes.

Inspired by: Minifig Journey Heads stop marker (#4) — "You're the minifig on this journey." Here, it's the engineer guiding the way.

Design notes
  • Classic minifig head (#F5CD2D) with red hard hat (#E3000B) — signals "engineer" / "builder" without complex detail
  • Hard hat = dome + brim, only two shapes — stays readable at 16px as a red blob on a yellow blob
  • Dot eyes with tiny white highlights add life without clutter — highlights vanish gracefully at small sizes
  • Confident smile suits the "presenting a plan" context — approachable and professional
  • No neck/body — just the head fills the square better as an icon and matches classic LEGO minifig head product imagery

Side by side — 32px (most common favicon size)

Mini-bricks

Abstract & universal. Says "LEGO" through shape alone. No face = more professional/neutral.

Engineer Head

Personal & story-driven. Says "builder" with character. More memorable and emotionally engaging.

Implementation

  • Chosen design goes into src/app/icon.svg — Next.js App Router serves it automatically via the file-based metadata API
  • Optionally add apple-icon.png (180×180) for iOS home screen — can be exported from the same SVG
  • SVG favicon is supported in all modern browsers and scales perfectly — no need for multiple .ico sizes
  • Both designs use only project design tokens (LEGO red, yellow, green) — visually consistent with the rest of the deck
